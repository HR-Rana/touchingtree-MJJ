Some Relationships Make You Question Everything

Am I crazy? Are they a narcissist? Or am I just overreacting?

So you might turn to Google or therapy to ask questions like, "Is this normal in a relationship?" or "Is my partner a narcissist?" and get lots of good information, but not the strategies you need to make things better.

Your relationship might even be good at times. Great, even! But then something happens that sets the high-conflict person off, and you find yourself in another fight seemingly over nothing...

Or you feel especially close to them for a while, but suddenly they become distant, and when you ask them about it they make you feel like nothing has changed and you’re going crazy.

Maybe you've even confronted this person in your life already... only to leave the conversation confused and feeling like you're the one in the wrong.

And there's a reason for this.

A High-Conflict Relationship Requires a
Different Set of Skills.

The skills you've learned that make other relationships successful won't work in a high conflict relationship.

You could be the most understanding, thoughtful, and kind partner, but that often won't be enough.

Sometimes the strategies and conversations that would work in a regular relationship might even backfire or make you feel like there’s something wrong with you.

But I want you to know there is hope. And I can say this with certainty because…

I've Been Through This Myself

I've been in high conflict relationships where I had no idea what was going on or going wrong.

So, like so many of you, I researched narcissism and found the same information you’ve probably found. I went to therapy. I joined chat rooms and support groups. I even tried talking to the high-conflict people about it. I thought if I explained to them that they had this problem they would understand, and the relationship would become better...

But nothing worked. I felt like I couldn't trust myself. All the confidence I used to have disappeared, and I doubted my ability to ever have healthy relationships.

But Things Can Get Better.

Because I didn’t find what to DO when I searched for narcissism, I eventually went to graduate school and learned all about these high-conflict individuals - like why they act the way they do, and how people like you and me can manage and get through these relationships in one piece..

I started working with clients who were also in high-conflict relationships, and I saw the same patterns over and over again. Their stories were similar to mine, and they were experiencing predictable behaviors, problems, and emotions.

And so I developed a program of strategies, frameworks, and skills for understanding and dealing with these high-conflict individuals and relationships.

It's Not Just About the Knowledge

Whether you're in a high conflict romantic relationship or the high-conflict person in your life is your parent, child, or friend... understanding them and why they act the way they do will help. But it's not enough.

The real key is having concrete and practical frameworks for things to say and ways to react, and a path to finding the strength, confidence, and self-love to create change or leave that relationship - if that's an option.

And that’s exactly what you get inside of this course. Here’s a peek at the process:

So. Much. Helping. But, that’s what calls me.

    It was crazy and a little exciting to me when I realized that all of my past life experiences: helping, teaching, marrying, parenting, divorcing, and writing were joining together to support my role as a therapist and, even more than that, as a therapist that specializes in high conflict relationships. This is not what my little kindergarten self imagined when she felt that first joy of being a helper! And yet, I’m so grateful to be here.

It turns out that knowing how, when, and why we should change our reactions to high conflict people can actually free us and allow us to manifest who we really want to be!
